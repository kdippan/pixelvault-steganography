What is Image Steganography?

Steganography is the practice of concealing a message within another ordinary file. PixelVault utilizes Least Significant Bit (LSB) manipulation. By reading the last bit of the color values of an image's pixels, we can rebuild the hidden binary data.

Military-Grade Security

PixelVault integrates the native Web Crypto API to reverse AES-GCM 256-bit encryption. If the hidden text was encrypted during the encoding process, it remains completely unreadable until you provide the exact decryption password.

100% Client-Side Processing

Unlike server-side extraction tools, PixelVault operates entirely within your browser utilizing the HTML5 Canvas API. Your extracted secrets and decryption passwords never leave your device.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my hidden message not extracting?

If the extraction fails, the image was likely compressed (e.g., sent via WhatsApp or saved as a JPEG). LSB steganography requires an exact, lossless PNG file. Also, ensure you have entered the correct AES password if the message was encrypted.

Is my extracted data sent to your servers?

No. PixelVault processes the decoding and decryption entirely within your browser. Nothing is uploaded, ensuring your extracted messages remain completely private.
